  • • Diabetes (insulin or non-insulin)
  • • Heart conditions or surgery
  • • Sleep apnea (bring CPAP data)
  • • Seizure history
  • • Vision impairment (bring ophthalmologist letter)
  • • Hearing loss (bring audiogram)

Contact us before your appointment if you have questions about specific conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a DOT medical certificate valid?

A DOT medical certificate is typically valid for up to 2 years. However, certain medical conditions (such as high blood pressure or diabetes) may result in a shorter certification period (usually 1 year) requiring more frequent exams.

Can I fail a DOT physical?

Yes, it is possible to not meet the medical standards. Common reasons include uncontrolled blood pressure (over 180/110), vision that cannot be corrected to 20/40, and hearing loss. We work with you to address concerns and explore exemptions when applicable.

Do I need to fast before the exam?

No fasting is required for a standard DOT physical. The urinalysis checks for glucose, protein, and other markers but does not require fasting. However, if your provider has ordered additional blood work, they may advise fasting.

Is the DOT physical the same as a drug test?

No. The DOT physical urinalysis tests for glucose, protein, and other health markers only. DOT drug testing is a separate process arranged by your employer. We do not perform DOT drug testing at this location.
